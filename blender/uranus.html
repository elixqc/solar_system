Uranus

An ice giant that turns on a tilted axis.

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. Its atmosphere contains methane, which gives it a soft blue-green color, and its axis is tilted so far that the planet appears to roll around the Sun.

Uranus summary

Uranus is an ice giant with a methane-rich atmosphere, faint rings, and a large axial tilt. Its unusual orientation gives it extreme seasons and a distinctive motion path around the Sun.

Key Features

What makes Uranus distinct.

Tilt

A planet that seems to roll.

Uranus is tilted by about 98 degrees, so its seasons are extreme and its poles experience long periods of sunlight or darkness.

Color

Blue-green from methane.

Methane in Uranus's upper atmosphere absorbs red light, leaving the planet with its cool blue-green appearance in photographs and renders.
